  General Notes:

Born in the New Weston Hotel, New York City, NY. No one anticipated twins, hence the second twin's legal name was "Coddington #2 Son" (NY State birth certificate 45611, City of New York). Name was changed to William Draper in legal proceeding (1/11/1943 deposition of Alice Muller Draper "In the matter of the Birth Certificate of William Draper Coddington").
After mother's death, reentered the United States on 24 Oct., 1922 at Ellis Island, New York, on ship Majestic, departed from Cherbourg, Mance, France, with brother Dave Hennen Jr. and grandmother Alice Draper.
First year of college tuition paid by family, but lost their fortune in crash of 1929 (29 Oct. 1929). Thereafter worked his way through college.
Received a letter from Selective Service Headquarters in Cooperstown, NY reclassifying him to "Class I," 16 Jan 1942.
Received a letter from Naval Aviation Cadet Selection Board in Brooklyn, NY offering a commission as Lieutenant A_V(S) (junior grade) in U.S. Naval Reserve.
Military Service: Bet. 1942-1945, U.S. Navy, discharged as Lt. Cmdr.

Worked as an estate manager, businessman, and stock broker. Died in a car accident Feb. 8, 1976. 24,108
